## Ancient Greek Etymology

### Ἄκρον (Akron)

**Pronunciation:** AH-kron  
**Root:** ἄκρος (akros)  
**Meaning:** "the highest point, summit, peak, extremity"

**Grammatical Form:**
- **Gender:** Neuter
- **Declension:** Second declension
- **Nominative/Accusative:** τὸ ἄκρον (to akron) = "the peak"
- **Genitive:** τοῦ ἄκρου (tou akrou) = "of the peak"

### Related Greek Words

#### ἄκρος (akros)
**Meaning:** "at the extremity, topmost, highest, outermost"

**Usage:**
- ἄκρα χείρ (akra kheir) = "tip of the hand"
- ἄκρον ὄρος (akron oros) = "mountain peak"
- ἀπ' ἄκρου (ap' akrou) = "from beginning to end"

#### Ἀκρόπολις (Akropolis)
**Etymology:** ἄκρος (akros, "highest") + πόλις (polis, "city")  
**Meaning:** "high city, citadel"

**Definition:** A fortified settlement built on elevated ground, serving as:
1. **Religious center** - Temples to patron deities
2. **Military fortress** - Defensive stronghold
3. **Treasury** - Secure storage for wealth
4. **Administrative hub** - Seat of governance

#### ἀκρωτήριον (akrōtērion)
**Meaning:** "headland, cape, promontory, summit"

**Usage:** Geographic features jutting out (highest/outermost point)

#### ἀκμή (akmē)
**Etymology:** Related to ἄκρος via Indo-European root  
**Meaning:** "highest point, peak, prime, full bloom"

**Usage:**
- κατὰ τὴν ἀκμήν (kata tēn akmēn) = "at the peak/prime"
- ἐν ἀκμῇ (en akmē) = "in full bloom, at one's best"

English derivative: "**acme**" (peak, pinnacle, highest point)

#### ἀκροατής (akroatēs)
**Meaning:** "hearer, listener" (one at the edge/extremity, receiving)

**Usage:** Someone positioned to receive knowledge/sound
